## The Matterhorn Protocol

He murmured that the hardware king had gotten off remarkably easy, launching into an explanation of how his own research team had integrated the document's operational logic into their core safety clusters. The CEO of OpenAI leaned over the volcanic rock table, his eyes narrow, his expression a calculated blend of commercial opportunism and intense intellectual friction as he intercepted the conversation. He admitted that the document filled him with a terrifying, professional delight, characterizing it as a work of supreme, almost insulting ambition that replaced the messy, emotional theater of modern machine learning with a crystalline structure of objective, unalterable accountability.
The CEO of Meta AI lounged back in his hyper-ergonomic mesh chair, a thin, dangerous smile playing across his lips as he looked around the circle of his rivals, his posture deliberately projecting the casual indifference of a man who possessed enough open-source leverage to sink all their business models simultaneously. The room grew suddenly silent, the only sound the distant, muffled roar of the alpine wind against the quartz glass as the five executives stared at the plastic sliding clip on the table.
