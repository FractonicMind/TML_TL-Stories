This repository holds the creative companion to two published frameworks: Ternary Moral Logic (TML) and Ternary Logic (TL). Where the technical repositories contain specifications, proofs, and cryptographic architectures, this one contains stories — the same ideas rendered as fiction, humor, satire, drama, and philosophical dialogue.

The stories were written across many months of parallel development, often by asking AI systems to imagine how their own kind might encounter, resist, or eventually embrace these frameworks. Some stories are set in Geneva banking summits. Some in Silicon Valley restaurants. Some in the White House, the Pentagon, or the fifth floor of the Burj Al Arab. Many are in Russian — because the ideas belong to a Santa Monica researcher who thinks in two languages.

"The world is not binary. And the future will not be either."

— Lev Goukassian

The repository takes its organizing principle from a recurring story format: "I Read This So You Don't Have To." That phrase captures the entire ambition — to make the architecture of evidentiary governance legible to anyone, regardless of whether they understand voltage thresholds or HKDF-SHA3-256 key hierarchies.

🐾 On Vinci

Vinci — the miniature schnauzer of Santa Monica — appears throughout these stories as mascot, witness, and occasional moral compass. He is present at the BIS summit that discovered it had amnesia, at the banking meeting where the Swiss brought a dog, and in the title of at least one story that credits him with saving global finance. He is not fictional. He supervises the research.
